Kolmogorov Theory takes two ideas as central — **computation** and **compression** — and asks what follows for agents embedded in a world. An algorithmic agent infers and runs compressive models of its sensorimotor stream, guided by Ockham's Razor: shorter programs that capture more regularity are preferred. The *structure* discovered in the data (its symmetries, its compositional and causal organization) is what gives first-person experience its spatial, temporal, and conceptual form.

Representative work:

- *An algorithmic information theory of consciousness* (Neuroscience of Consciousness, 2017)
- *AIT Foundations of Structured Experience* (Journal of AI and Consciousness, 2022)
- *Structured Dynamics in the Algorithmic Agent* (Entropy, 2025)
- *Compositional Symmetry as Compression: Lie Pseudogroup Structure in Algorithmic Agents* (2025)

KT is now the core research program of the Barcelona Computational Foundation.
